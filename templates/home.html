Types of Crop Stress

Water Stress

Drought or excess water affects plant growth and reduces crop yield.

Temperature Stress

Extreme heat or cold interferes with plant metabolic processes.

Soil & Chemical Stress

Nutrient deficiency or pH imbalance weakens plant development.

Light & Atmospheric Stress

Changes in sunlight, wind or humidity disturb plant health.

Parameters Used

SAVI

Vegetation index that measures plant health while minimizing soil effects.

NDVI

Indicates vegetation vigor and photosynthesis activity.

Temperature

Ambient temperature affecting crop metabolic processes.

Humidity

Controls plant transpiration and moisture balance.

Rainfall

Impacts soil water availability and irrigation requirements.

Soil Moisture

Water present in soil necessary for root absorption.

Soil pH

Determines nutrient availability in soil.

Organic Matter

Improves soil fertility and water retention.

Water Flow

Monitors irrigation distribution across farmland.

About AgriAI

AgriAI is an intelligent crop monitoring system that predicts crop stress using machine learning models trained on agricultural parameters.

The system analyzes environmental and soil conditions to detect stress early and recommend corrective actions for farmers.

Users can analyze field conditions, detect crop stress, and visualize prediction history through interactive dashboards.
